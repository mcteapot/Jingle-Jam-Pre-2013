ABOUT HOLIDAY GAME JAM
It’s the most wonderful time of the year! Holiday Game Jam invites game developers to create games that celebrate the optimism, thankfulness, and love of the holiday season. Developers work over December creating games in this spirit. Each day, we will unveil a new in-progress game on our homepage. Then, December 25th, you’ll be able to donate as much or as little as you can to charity, in exchange for a bounty of fine videogame gifts!

The jam happens anytime, anywhere during December. Developers just sign up online, submit info about what they’re making, then submit it for release. We’ll be coordinating holiday parties, potlucks, movie screenings, and more that double as game jam locations throughout the month.

PLAYERS
Sign up for email updates to stay informed about the jam, and local events popping up during it!

Follow Holiday Game Jam on Twitter for the most livetweety information!

Check in during December to see and read about games in-progress!

Return December 25th for your presents! Pay what you can, and you’ll get all the games! All money will be given to a charity of your choosin.


DEVELOPERS
What you do
Get together with your friends and make a game that celebrates the holiday spirit. Be as traditional or as punk rock as you want in style and tone, but please, stick to the theme of joy and kindness. No Santa-Shooting Simulator or Giant Menorah Monster Z.

When you do it
Whenever you want during December! Spend as much or as little time as you can spare to make something kids and adults would want to play.

* 11/29 - Registration Open. Submit an image, title, description, and team info of the game you’re working on.

* 12/1 - Daily Features Begin. We’ll begin featuring one game per day on the homepage. The sooner you submit your game concept, the more chances you’ll have to be featured!

* 12/22 - Game Submissions Due by 11:59pm PST. You’ll submit a link (sorry, we can’t host) for players to download your game.

* 12/25 - RELEASE DAY. We’ll do a massive announcement of all games submitted to press and the public, and make the games available as a big bundle on the site.

Where you do it
The holidays are all around us, so work wherever you’d like! We’ll make announcements as we put together a schedule of events that you can attend to meet collaborators or just jam alongside other teams.

How you do it
Teams can be as big or small as you like.
Use any tools you like.
Start from scratch or work on an (unreleased!) game you’ve been tinkering with.

Why you do it
To spread cheer and love during the holidays. We want games of a positive nature. Gore, hate, and sarcasm need not apply. But if you want to make a Die Hard game, that’s cool. That’s a delightful holiday movie!
